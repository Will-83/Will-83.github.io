On The Air

THE WORKBENCH & PROJECTS

Welcome to my build log! This is where I document my amateur radio and maker projects. I love combining 3D printing (using PETG on my Klipper-powered Ender 3) with radio hardware, antenna building, and emergency communications gear. Check out the details of my current and completed projects below.

1. Homebrew 10m-40m Linked Dipole

Status: Fully Operational / Field Tested

Designed for fast deployment during POTA (Parks on the Air) activations and emergency field operations. This dipole covers 10, 15, 20, and 40 meters by manually opening or closing Anderson Powerpole connectors at calculated wire links.

Key Build Details:
  • Choke: Custom FT-240-31 common-mode toroidal choke to stop feedline radiation.
  • Hardware: 3D printed center insulator and link dogbones printed in high-temp, UV-resistant black PETG.
  • Wire: 18 AWG Silky ultra-flexible copper-clad steel wire for tangle-free deployment.
Custom 1:1 common-mode choke and PETG wire links.

2. Weatherproof Meshtastic Home Gateway

Status: Deployment Stage

To establish a permanent local footprint in the Ozarks mesh, I am building a fixed MQTT home gateway node mounted high up on an outdoor mast to maximize coverage.

Key Build Details:
  • Hardware: Heltec V4 LoRa board running on 915 MHz.
  • Enclosure: Weatherproof IP67 outdoor junction box with customized internal 3D-printed brackets to secure the board and battery.
  • Power: Powered via a Power-over-Ethernet (PoE) split line or solar/LiFePO4 battery array for uninterrupted uptime.
The Heltec V4 core module ready for weatherproofing.

3. Custom Mobile Digital Hotspot

Status: Design Phase

To maintain seamless digital voice access (DMR, YSF, and NXDN) while operating mobile in the truck, I am designing a vehicle-integrated digital voice hotspot.

Key Build Details:
  • Hotspot Core: Raspberry Pi Zero 2W coupled with a duplex MMDVM hat tucked away securely behind the vehicle dash.
  • Display Interface: A wired 3.2-inch Nextion TFT display screen flush-mounted on the dash console for real-time status, callsign, and talkgroup monitoring.
  • Network: Automating local Wi-Fi tethering to my phone's cellular hotspot upon ignition.
Conceptualizing the custom dash-mount enclosure on the workbench.
